How NTA Score/Percentile is Calculated

The percentile score indicates the relative performance of a candidate compared to all others who appeared in the same shift. It is NOT the raw percentage of marks obtained.

Step 1: Raw Marks Calculation

First, raw scores are computed. Every correct question adds 4 marks. Every incorrect answer deducts 1 mark. Unattempted questions yield 0 marks. The maximum possible raw score is 300.

Step 2: Equi-Percentile Normalization

NTA applies normalization separately for each shift. The formula is:
Percentile = 100 × (No. of candidates in shift with Raw Score ≤ your score) / (Total candidates in shift) This scale ranges from 100 down to 0, matching the exact relative position of the candidate in the shift.

Step 3: Multi-Session Resolution

Since candidates write in different sessions, NTA merges all session records at the end. Your final merit rank (CRL) is derived from the higher percentile score obtained between the January and April sessions.

Why Marks vs Percentile Fluctuates by Shift

Many students wonder why scoring 180 marks yields a 99 percentile in one session, but only a 97 percentile in another. This is due to two critical variables:

1. Shift Difficulty Disparities

If a shift has an exceptionally difficult Mathematics or Physics section, the average raw score of all candidates in that shift decreases. Consequently, candidates require fewer raw marks to beat 99% of the peer group. On easy papers, the benchmark rises significantly.

2. Session Size and Candidate Profile Distribution

NTA splits 16,00,000+ candidates randomly into multiple sessions. While NTA uses random grouping algorithms, the concentration of high-scoring coaching students can sometimes fluctuate between shifts, introducing subtle variances in the competition baseline.

JEE Main Percentile FAQs

Everything you need to know about the NTA normalisation and expected percentile curves.

What is the formula NTA uses for percentile score?
The percentile is calculated shift-wise: Percentile = 100 × (Number of candidates who appeared in the session with raw marks equal to or less than you) / (Total number of candidates in that session). Ranks are based on the final combined percentiles.
What is the difference between percentage and percentile?
Percentage is the score calculated out of the maximum marks (e.g., scoring 150/300 is 50%). Percentile represents the percentage of candidates who scored lower than or equal to you. A 99 percentile means you performed better than 99% of the candidates in that shift, regardless of your marks.
Is the percentile calculated separately for Mathematics, Physics, and Chemistry?
Yes, NTA calculates and prints individual percentile scores for Math, Physics, and Chemistry, alongside the overall composite percentile. These individual percentiles are used to break ties if two candidates finish with the same overall percentile.
