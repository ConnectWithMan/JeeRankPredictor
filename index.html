JEE 2027 Admission & Counselling Guide

A step-by-step walkthrough of the seat allocation cycle for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs.

1

Exam & Results Declaration

Candidates appear for the JEE Main (Session 1 & 2) and/or JEE Advanced exams. NTA declares shift-wise normalized percentile scores, and subsequently publishes the Final All India Rank (AIR) list based on the best of two JEE Main session percentiles. The top ~2.5 Lakh candidates qualify for JEE Advanced.

2

JoSAA Portal Registration & Choice Filling

All qualified candidates register on the official JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) portal. Here, candidates must lock their preferences for engineering colleges and branches. Option-filling is a critical step; candidates must enter choices in order of absolute preference, as reallocation only happens upward.

3

Mock Seat Allocation & Rounds

JoSAA releases mock seat allocations to help students gauge their probability. Next, actual seat allocation happens over multiple rounds (typically 5-6 rounds). When a seat is allocated, the candidate must choose one of three options: Freeze (accept and lock), Slide (accept but look for higher branch in the same institute), or Float (accept but look for higher branch/institute).

4

Document Verification & Seat Acceptance

Once a seat is accepted, candidates upload required academic and category documents (like Category Certificate, Class 10/12 marksheets, Medical Certificate) and pay the seat acceptance fee. Verification is conducted online by the virtual reporting center of the allotted institute.

5

CSAB Special Rounds (For NITs, IIITs & GFTIs)

After JoSAA rounds conclude, any vacant seats remaining in NITs, IIITs, and other Government Funded Technical Institutes (GFTIs) are filled via two CSAB (Central Seat Allocation Board) Special Rounds. CSAB rounds often see cutoffs drop, giving a second chance to students with lower percentiles.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Everything you need to know about JEE Main 2027 normalizations, calculations, and college selections.

How does JEE Main normalization work?
Since the exam is conducted in multiple shifts with varying difficulty, NTA uses a normalization process based on percentile scores. Your percentile represents the percentage of candidates who scored equal to or less than you in that specific shift. It is calculated as: 100 × (No. of candidates in the shift with score ≤ raw score) / (Total candidates in the shift).
What is a good percentile in JEE Main 2027?
A percentile above 99 is considered excellent and generally guarantees a Computer Science (CSE) seat in top-tier NITs and IIITs. Percentiles between 95 and 98 qualify you for middle and lower-tier NITs/IIITs or core branches. A percentile above 93-94 (depending on category) is typically required to qualify for JEE Advanced.
How are Category Ranks estimated?
Category ranks (OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST) are estimated from your predicted All India Rank (AIR) using historical allocation curves and the reservation distribution ratios defined by the Government of India. In counselling portals like JoSAA, category cutoffs are displayed using Category Ranks.
What is the difference between JoSAA and CSAB?
JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) conducts the first 6 rounds of counselling for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. After JoSAA rounds are completed, CSAB (Central Seat Allocation Board) conducts special rounds to fill the remaining vacant seats in NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs (IIT seats are not part of CSAB). CSAB cutoffs are often slightly more relaxed.
