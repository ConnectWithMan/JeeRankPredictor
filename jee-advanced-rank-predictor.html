JEE Advanced Marks vs. Rank Trends

Unlike JEE Main, where percentiles are calculated shift-wise, JEE Advanced is evaluated on absolute score totals. Since paper difficulty varies significantly each year, the marks required to clear cutoffs and secure top ranks fluctuate.

1. Cumulative Score Thresholds

To be included in the Common Rank List (CRL), candidates must satisfy both aggregate score requirements (typically around 8-10% of total marks for General) and individual subject cutoffs in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (typically 4-5% of subject totals).

2. IIT Preparatory Courses

For vacant reserved category seats (SC, ST, PwD), IITs conduct preparatory courses. Candidates who do not meet the direct qualification cutoffs but clear relaxed bounds are eligible for a 1-year preparatory training program at designated IITs, after which they are admitted directly into their undergraduate course.

How is JEE Advanced Rank Calculated?

Unlike JEE Main, which normalizes scores using equi-percentile mathematical curves, JEE Advanced rankings are determined by raw aggregate marks scored across Paper 1 and Paper 2.

1. Aggregate Score and Subject-Wise Cutoffs

To qualify for rank allocation, a candidate must satisfy two conditions simultaneously:

  • Subject-wise Cutoff: You must score a minimum percentage of marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics individually. This is usually around 4% to 5% of the total marks in each subject.
  • Aggregate Cutoff: You must clear a minimum aggregate marks percentage across both papers combined (typically around 8% to 10% for the OPEN category, and lower for OBC, SC, ST, EWS).

JEE Advanced Rank FAQs

Answers to common questions about aggregate cutoffs, tie-breakers, and category listings.

What happens if I clear the aggregate cutoff but fail one subject cutoff in JEE Advanced?
If you fail to clear the individual cutoff in even one of the three subjects (Physics, Chemistry, or Math), you will NOT be included in the rank list, regardless of how high your overall aggregate score is.
How are ties resolved in the JEE Advanced rank list?
If two or more candidates score the same aggregate marks, the candidate with higher marks in Mathematics is awarded the higher rank. If the tie persists, the candidate with higher marks in Physics is ranked higher. If the tie still remains, both candidates are awarded the same rank.
What marks are typically needed for an IIT rank under 5,000?
This depends heavily on the year's difficulty, but historically, a score of 45% to 50% of the total marks (approx. 160-180 marks out of 360) is required to secure a rank under 5,000, ensuring choice branches in top IITs.
