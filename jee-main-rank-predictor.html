Common Rank List (CRL) vs. Category Ranks

When the National Testing Agency (NTA) declares the JEE Main results, every candidate receives a Common Rank List (CRL) rank. Candidates belonging to reserved categories (OBC-NCL, EWS, SC, ST) also receive a Category Rank.

1. How is CRL Rank calculated?

Your CRL is based on your overall percentile score relative to all unique candidates who appeared in both January and April sessions. For 2027, the unique candidate count is projected to reach 16,00,000.

2. How are seats allocated in JoSAA?

During JoSAA seat allocation, if you belong to a reserved category, you are first evaluated for general seats using your CRL. If those are unavailable, you are evaluated for reserved seats in your category using your Category Rank. Hence, your category rank is a direct measure of your admission chances.

Understanding JEE Main Rank Calculation

The All India Rank (AIR) in JEE Main is calculated by combining normalized scores from all shifts. Because candidate counts and paper difficulty fluctuate, raw marks alone cannot determine rankings. NTA merges January and April sessions into a single master list using candidate percentiles.

Official Subject-Wise Tie-Breaker Rules (2027 Guidelines)

If two or more candidates obtain the exact same overall NTA Percentile Score, NTA applies the following tie-breaking criteria in strict sequential order:

  1. Mathematics Percentile: Candidate with the higher percentile score in Mathematics is ranked higher.
  2. Physics Percentile: If the tie remains, the candidate with the higher percentile score in Physics is ranked higher.
  3. Chemistry Percentile: If the tie remains, the candidate with the higher percentile score in Chemistry is ranked higher.
  4. Incorrect-to-Correct Ratio: Candidate with a lower proportion of incorrect answers relative to correct answers across all subjects is ranked higher.
  5. Age/Application Number: Older candidates were historically favored, but NTA currently defaults to ascending order of application numbers if all other parameters are identical.

JEE Main Rank FAQs

Common questions about expected marks, ranks, and JOSAA seat allocation categories.

What is a Common Rank List (CRL) rank?
The Common Rank List (CRL) rank is your Open category rank. It includes all candidates who appeared for the exam across India, sorted by their best NTA percentile score. Regardless of whether you belong to a reserved category (OBC, SC, ST, EWS), you will always receive a CRL rank.
How does a Category Rank help during admission?
Reserved category seats in NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs are allocated based on your Category Rank. During JoSAA counselling, the seat allocation algorithm first checks if you qualify for a seat under the General (OPEN) quota using your CRL. If not, it uses your Category Rank to allocate a seat within your category's reserved seat pool.
What marks are needed for a rank under 10,000 in JEE Main 2027?
To secure a rank under 10,000 (which corresponds to roughly a 99.35+ percentile), you generally need a raw score of 180 to 200+ marks in moderate shifts, and 165+ in harder shifts. Easy shifts might require 210+ marks.
