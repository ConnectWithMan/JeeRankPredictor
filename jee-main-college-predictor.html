Home State (HS) vs. Other State (OS) Quotas

National Institutes of Technology (NITs) allocate 50% of their total seats under the Home State Quota. The remaining 50% are filled via the Other State Quota.

Home State Quota eligibility

Your Home State is determined by the location of the school where you completed your Class 12 (or equivalent) board examination. It has nothing to do with your place of birth or residence. For example, if you live in Delhi but passed Class 12 in Uttar Pradesh, your Home State is Uttar Pradesh.

Impact on Cutoff Ranks

In most states (with exceptions like Delhi or Goa where competition is fierce), the closing ranks for the Home State Quota are significantly higher (more relaxed) than the Other State Quota. Choosing your Home State option inside the predictor lets you see these options.

JoSAA vs. CSAB Counselling Mechanics

Admissions to public engineering institutes in India happen in two successive phases: JoSAA rounds and CSAB special rounds.

JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority)

JoSAA conducts the first 5-6 rounds of counselling for all 23 IITs, 31 NITs, 26 IIITs, and 30+ GFTIs. Every student registers once and locks their preference list. Category seats and gender-neutral/female-only supernumerary seats are allocated strictly by merit in successive rounds.

CSAB Special Rounds

After JoSAA rounds conclude, any seat vacant in NITs, IIITs, or GFTIs due to withdrawals or empty allocations are transferred to the Central Seat Allocation Board (CSAB). CSAB conducts 2 Special Rounds. Note: **IIT seats are NOT part of CSAB rounds.**

College Predictor FAQs

Answers to common questions about cutoffs, seat quotas, and branch options.

What is a mock seat allocation?
Before the actual rounds begin, JoSAA runs two mock seat allocation rounds based on the initial choices locked by students. This helps you understand what college and branch you are likely to get, allowing you to reorder your choices before the final deadline.
Can I get into a top NIT with 95 percentile?
Under the General category (OPEN), getting a top branch (like CSE/ECE) in top-tier NITs (like NIT Trichy or NIT Surathkal) is very difficult at 95 percentile. However, you can secure seats in lower branches (like Civil, Metallurgy, or Biotechnology) in those NITs, or core branches in newer NITs and GFTIs, especially if you qualify for the Home State Quota.
What is a GFTI?
GFTIs (Government Funded Technical Institutes) are public engineering colleges funded by the Central Government or State Governments (such as BIT Mesra, PEC Chandigarh, and JNU School of Engineering). They participate in JoSAA/CSAB counselling and accept admissions via JEE Main ranks.
